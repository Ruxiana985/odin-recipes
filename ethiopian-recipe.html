Recipe list for the flavorful ethiopian cuisine

Ethiopian cuisine is a flavorful journey through history. With ancient roots tracing back over 3,000 years to the Aksumite Empire, it’s built on teff, a tiny grain used to make injera, a spongy flatbread that’s a staple of every meal.
The cuisine features berbere, a spicy blend of chili peppers, garlic, and other spices, which adds a unique kick to dishes. Religious practices also shape the cuisine, leading to a rich variety of vegetarian dishes.
Ethiopian food is a vibrant mix of tradition, flavor, and communal dining.

    DoroWot Recipe
  • INGREDIENTS
    • 4 chicken thighs (bone-in, skinless)
    • 2 tablespoons niter kibbeh (Ethiopian spiced butter) or regular butter
    • 2 large onions, finely chopped
    • 4 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
    • 2 tablespoons berbere spice mix (Ethiopian chili spice blend)
    • 1 can (14.5 oz) crushed tomatoes
    • 2 cups chicken broth
    • 4 hard-boiled eggs, peeled
    • Salt to taste
    • Fresh cilantro (optional, for garnish)
  • STEPS
    1. Rinse the chicken thighs and pat them dry. Optionally, you can season them with a bit of salt and let them marinate for a few hours.
    2. Heat the niter kibbeh or butter in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onions and cook, stirring frequently, until they are deeply caramelized and golden brown, about 10-15 minutes.
    3. Stir in the minced garlic and ginger and cook for another 2-3 minutes until fragrant.
    4. Mix in the berbere spice, cooking for 1-2 minutes until the spices are well combined with the onions.
    5. Stir in the crushed tomatoes and cook for 5 minutes, allowing the mixture to thicken
    6. Add the chicken broth and bring to a simmer. Add the chicken thighs and cook for about 40-45 minutes, or until the chicken is tender and cooked through.
    7. Gently add the hard-boiled eggs to the stew, allowing them to warm through and absorb some of the flavors, about 10 minutes.
    8. Taste and adjust the seasoning with salt as needed. Garnish with fresh cilantro if desired. Serve with injera (Ethiopian flatbread)

    Misir Recipe
  • INGREDIENTS
    • 2 cup red lentils, rinsed and drained
    • 3 tablespoons niter kibbeh (Ethiopian spiced butter) or regular butter
    • 2 large onions, finely chopped
    • 4 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
    • 4 tablespoons berbere spice mix (Ethiopian chili spice blend)
    • 3 tablespoons tomato paste
    • 3 cups water
    • Black pepper to taste
    • Salt to taste
    • Fresh cilantro
  • STEPS
    1. Niter Kibbeh is a spiced clarified butter. To make it, melt unsalted butter in a saucepan, add spices like cardamom, cinnamon, garlic, and ginger, and simmer on low until the butter is infused with flavors. Strain the spices and set the clarified butter aside.
    2. Heat the niter kibbeh or butter in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onions and cook, stirring frequently, until they are deeply caramelized and golden brown, about 10-15 minutes.
    3. Stir in the minced garlic and ginger and cook for another 2-3 minutes until fragrant.
    4. Add the berbere spice blend to the pot and cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly. This step is crucial to bloom the spices and enhance their flavor. Be careful not to burn them.
    5. Stir in the tomato paste and cook for another 2 minutes to deepen the flavor. Add the rinsed lentils to the pot and mix well to coat them with the spices.
    6. Pour in the water or vegetable broth and bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce the heat to low and let it simmer uncovered for about 20-25 minutes, or until the lentils are tender and the stew has thickened. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking and ensure even cooking.
    7. Add salt and black pepper to taste. If the stew is too thick, add a little more water or broth to reach your desired consistency.
    8. Taste and adjust the seasoning with salt as needed. Garnish with fresh cilantro if desired. Serve with injera (Ethiopian flatbread)
    Shiro Recipe
  • INGREDIENTS
    • 1 cup shiro powder (available at Ethiopian or international grocery stores)
    • 2 tablespoons niter kibbeh (Ethiopian spiced butter) or regular butter
    • 1 large onion, finely chopped
    • 4 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
    • 2 tablespoons berbere spice mix (adjust based on spice preference)
    • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
    • 3 cups vegetable broth or water
    • Salt to taste
    • Fresh cilantro (optional, for garnish)
  • STEPS
    1. Heat the niter kibbeh or butter in a pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and cook until they are golden brown and caramelized, about 10-15 minutes.
    2. Stir in the minced garlic and ginger and cook for another 2-3 minutes until fragrant.
    3. Mix in the berbere spice, cooking for 1-2 minutes to release the flavors.
    4. Stir in the diced tomatoes and cook for 5 minutes, allowing the mixture to thicken
    5. Gradually stir in the shiro powder, mixing well to avoid lumps. Cook for 2-3 minutes, allowing the flavors to combine.
    6. Slowly add the vegetable broth or water, stirring continuously to achieve a smooth consistency. Bring to a simmer.
    7. Let the mixture simmer for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally. The shiro should thicken to a stew-like consistency. Adjust the thickness by adding more liquid if needed.
    8. Finally,serve it with injera and a little tip from me for killing fragrance and taste, add melted butter in your serving bowl
